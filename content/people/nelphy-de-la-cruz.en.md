Nelphy De la Cruz Félix is a Ph.D. in Physics specializing in Statistical Mechanics and Computational Simulation, with a focus on the adsorption of flexible objects on lattices, percolation phenomena, and surface growth. As a National Career Researcher attached to MESCYT and a professor at the Autonomous University of Santo Domingo (UASD), she leads research projects utilizing Monte Carlo methods and Density Functional Theory (DFT), having authored pioneering publications in *Physical Review E* in the Dominican Republic. Her academic work is complemented by strong institutional engagement, serving as the coordinator of the Statistical Physics group for the Dominican Physics Society (SoDoFi) and Vice Director of the OWSD chapter in the Dominican Republic, where she actively promotes open science and the advancement of women in science.
