The UASD Institute of Physics is proud to announce the official opening of the state-of-the-art neutrino telescope, a milestone that marks a new chapter in high-energy particle research. The research team led by Dr. Vladimir Pérez and Dr. Ana Sánchez has worked for the past few years on the design and construction of this instrument, which is now ready to begin collecting data and advancing knowledge about the nature of neutrinos and their interactions with the terrestrial environment.

The telescope, installed in the university's high-tech laboratory, incorporates state-of-the-art detection technology that will allow the observation of neutrino events with unprecedented precision. The results are expected to contribute significantly to particle physics, astroparticle physics, and cosmology, offering new opportunities for international collaboration.

The scientific community, students, and the general public are invited to attend the inauguration ceremony, which will include presentations of research projects and real-time demonstrations of the telescope's capabilities. The UASD thanks all collaborators, institutions, and sponsors who made this achievement possible and is committed to continuing to advance cutting-edge scientific research.
