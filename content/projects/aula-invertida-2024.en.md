### Abstract

This project aims to implement the model of the flipped classroom as an innovation in physics education
in higher education. The digital and pedagogical transformation within the educational community has
given rise to new paradigms, and the flipped classroom has proven to be a successful model within the
context of blended learning (b-learning).This study is justified by the need for universities to become
spaces of innovation to address the changing needs of the education. The research is based on national
and international frameworks, such as the National Development Strategy (END-2030) and the
Sustainable Development Goals 2030, which promote inclusive, equitable, and quality education.
Furthermore, this study responds to the FONDOCYT 2023 call, which aims to support proposals for
transformation in higher education and the improvement of educational quality. It ill employ a mixed-
methods design and will be conducted with students enrolled in Basic Physics at the Autonomous
University of Santo Domingo. This research will utilize a hermeneutic-interpretive and critical qualitative
approach, as well as a quantitative approach within an empirical-positivist paradigm. A quasi-experimental design with a control group and an experimental group will be employed. The instructional
design will incorporate a combination of the ADDIE-Flipped Classroom models. The expected outcomes
include a positive impact on the academic performance of physics students and the development of
competencies in the field. Additionally, the results will be shared with the scientific community and
utilized to evaluate the effectiveness of the flipped classroom model within the Dominican context.
