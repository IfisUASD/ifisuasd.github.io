# Project Description

The **MESCYT-2024-02** project focused on the detection and study of supernovae in the Andean sky, taking advantage of high-resolution telescopes located in the Andes mountain range. Its objective was to improve knowledge of stellar evolution and the role of supernovae in the dispersion of heavy elements in the universe.

## Methodology

- Installation and calibration of large-aperture telescopes in areas with low light pollution.
- Systematic observation of regions with high densities of star clusters.
- Use of data reduction algorithms and automatic detection of supernova events.
- Collaboration with international observatories to confirm and classify detected events.

## Results

- 12 Type II supernovae and 3 Type Ia supernovae were identified and cataloged.
- 4 articles were published in high-impact international journals.
- An open-source data processing pipeline was developed that will be used by the astrophysics community.

## Impact

- Contribution to knowledge about the evolution of massive stars and the dispersion of heavy elements.
- Strengthening of scientific infrastructure in the Andean region.
- Training of undergraduate and postgraduate students in astronomical data observation and analysis techniques.
